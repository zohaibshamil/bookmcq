Q1. A software developer in 1983 wishes to distribute a compiler that allows users to modify and redistribute the code, provided any derived works remain free. Which licensing mechanism best supports this goal?

🔹 A. Public Domain dedication
🔹 B. Copyright with no license
🔹 C. BSD License
🔹 D. GNU General Public License (GPL)

💡 Difficulty: easy | ✅ Correct: D

Q2. During the early 1980s, a university student observes that proprietary UNIX licenses are becoming restrictive and expensive. He decides to create a free, UNIX-compatible operating system from scratch. Who is this individual?

🔹 A. Linus Torvalds
🔹 B. Richard Stallman
🔹 C. Ken Thompson
🔹 D. Dennis Ritchie

💡 Difficulty: medium | ✅ Correct: B

Q3. A corporation in the late 1990s faces a lawsuit claiming its operating system bundles too many features, stifling competition. This event highlights the tension between which two concepts?

🔹 A. Open-source development and proprietary control
🔹 B. Monolithic kernel design and microkernel design
🔹 C. Free software and open-source hardware
🔹 D. User space and kernel space

💡 Difficulty: hard | ✅ Correct: A
