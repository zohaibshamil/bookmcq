Q1. A university computing lab has 40 students submitting jobs simultaneously to a single CPU. The scheduler must decide which job executes next. What is the primary objective of CPU scheduling in this environment?

🔹 A. To allocate equal memory to all processes
🔹 B. To maximize CPU utilization by keeping the processor busy
🔹 C. To minimize the number of processes in memory
🔹 D. To prioritize disk I/O operations over CPU operations

💡 Difficulty: easy | ✅ Correct: B

Q2. Which scheduling algorithm is mathematically proven to minimize average waiting time when all processes arrive simultaneously?

🔹 A. First-Come First-Served (FCFS)
🔹 B. Round Robin (RR)
🔹 C. Shortest Job First (SJF)
🔹 D. Priority Scheduling

💡 Difficulty: medium | ✅ Correct: C

Q3. Given the following FCFS schedule with arrival times: P1(0,4), P2(1,3), P3(2,2), P4(3,5). What is the average waiting time?
