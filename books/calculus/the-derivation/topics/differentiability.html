Q1. A function \(f\) is differentiable at \(x_0\) if:

🔹 A. \(\lim_{h\to 0} \frac{f(x_0+h)-f(x_0)}{h}\) exists
🔹 B. \(\lim_{x\to x_0} f(x)\) exists
🔹 C. \(f\) is continuous at \(x_0\)
🔹 D. \(f(x_0)\) is defined

💡 Difficulty: easy | ✅ Correct: A

Q2. Is \(f(x) = x^{2/3}\) differentiable at \(x=0\)?

🔹 A. No, it has a cusp
🔹 B. Yes, derivative is 0
🔹 C. No, it's discontinuous
🔹 D. Yes, derivative exists

💡 Difficulty: medium | ✅ Correct: A
