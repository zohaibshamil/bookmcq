📝 Sample Questions

Q1. Consider \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\) on the interval \([0,4]\). Which statement correctly applies the EVT?

🔹 A. \(f\) attains its maximum at \(x=0\).
🔹 B. \(f\) attains its minimum at \(x=4\).
🔹 C. \(f\) attains its maximum at \(x=4\) and its minimum at \(x=0\).
🔹 D. \(f\) does not satisfy the EVT because it is not differentiable at \(x=0\).

💡 Difficulty: easy | ✅ Correct: C

Q2. If a function is continuous on \([a,b]\) but not differentiable at a point \(c\in(a,b)\), what does the EVT say about the existence of extrema at \(c\)?

🔹 A. \(c\) cannot be an extremum because differentiability is required.
🔹 B. \(c\) may be an absolute extremum even if the derivative does not exist there.
🔹 C. The EVT does not apply because differentiability fails.
🔹 D. Only local extrema can occur at \(c\).

💡 Difficulty: medium | ✅ Correct: B

Q3. In a proof of the EVT using the completeness of \(\mathbb{R}\), which theorem is typically invoked to locate a point where the supremum is attained?

🔹 A. Bolzano–Weierstrass Theorem
🔹 B. Mean Value Theorem
🔹 C. Intermediate Value Theorem
🔹 D. Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

💡 Difficulty: hard | ✅ Correct: A
