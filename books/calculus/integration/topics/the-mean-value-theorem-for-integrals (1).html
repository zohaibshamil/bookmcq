Q1. According to the Mean Value Theorem for Integrals, what does the number \(c\) represent for a continuous function \(f\) on \([a,b]\)?

🔹 A. A point where \(f(c)\) equals the average value of \(f\) on \([a,b]\)
🔹 B. The maximum value of \(f\) on \([a,b]\)
🔹 C. The minimum value of \(f\) on \([a,b]\)
🔹 D. The point where the derivative \(f'(c)\) is zero

💡 Difficulty: easy | ✅ Correct: A

Q2. A dragster travels from \(t = 0\) to \(t = 4\) seconds with velocity \(v(t) = 4 - t\) (m/s). Using the Mean Value Theorem for Integrals, which statement is true?

🔹 A. There exists a time \(c\) where \(v(c)\) equals the average speed over the interval
🔹 B. The maximum speed occurs at \(t = 0\)
🔹 C. The minimum speed occurs at \(t = 4\)
🔹 D. The average speed is 2 m/s

💡 Difficulty: medium | ✅ Correct: A

🔹 A. 6
