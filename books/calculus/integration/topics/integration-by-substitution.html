Q2. A dragster's velocity is given by \(v(t) = 4t^3 - 2t\) (meters per second). Using substitution, find the distance traveled from \(t = 0\) to \(t = 2\). Which integral correctly represents the distance?

🔹 A. \(\int_0^2 (12t^2 - 2) \, dt\)
🔹 B. \(\int_0^2 (12t^2 - 2t) \, dt\)
🔹 C. \(\int_0^2 (12t^2 - 2) \, dt\)
🔹 D. \(\int_0^2 (12t^2 - 2) \, dt\)

💡 Difficulty: medium | ✅ Correct: C
