Q1. What does the definite integral \(\int_a^b f(x) \, dx\) represent geometrically?

🔹 A. The slope of the tangent line at a point
🔹 B. The net signed area between the curve \(f(x)\) and the x‑axis from \(a\) to \(b\)
🔹 C. The average value of \(f\) on \([a,b]\)
🔹 D. The antiderivative of \(f\) evaluated at \(b\)

💡 Difficulty: easy | ✅ Correct: B

🔹 A. Applying the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus to the antiderivative \(\frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}\)
🔹 B. Using integration by parts repeatedly
🔹 C. Approximating the integral with Riemann sums and taking the limit
🔹 D. Differentiating \(x^{n+1}\) and then integrating

💡 Difficulty: hard | ✅ Correct: C
