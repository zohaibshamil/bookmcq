📝 Sample Questions

Q1. If \(f\) and \(g\) are continuous at \(c\), then \(f+g\) is:

🔹 A. Continuous at \(c\)
🔹 B. Discontinuous at \(c\)
🔹 C. Undefined at \(c\)
🔹 D. Differentiable at \(c\)

💡 Difficulty: easy | ✅ Correct: A

Q2. In Theorem 1.5.3(d), if \(g(c)=0\), what happens to \(f/g\)?

🔹 A. It is continuous
🔹 B. It has a discontinuity at \(c\)
🔹 C. It is always 0
🔹 D. It is undefined at \(c\)

💡 Difficulty: medium | ✅ Correct: B

Q3. If \(f\) and \(g\) are continuous at \(c\) and \(f(c)=g(c)=0\), then the quotient \(f/g\):

🔹 A. Is always continuous at \(c\)
🔹 B. Is always discontinuous at \(c\)
🔹 C. May be continuous or discontinuous
🔹 D. Is undefined

💡 Difficulty: hard | ✅ Correct: C
